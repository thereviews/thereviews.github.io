<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>NVR Basics</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What Is an NVR?</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>A Network Video Recorder (NVR) records video from IP cameras over a network. Unlike DVRs that rely on analog cameras, NVRs support:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Higher resolutions (4K and beyond)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Remote monitoring via apps</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Smart AI detection</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Easier expansion</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Cleaner PoE installations</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In 2026, most new security systems are IP-based, making NVRs the modern standard.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Key Terms You Should Know</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>PoE (Power over Ethernet)</strong><br>A single Ethernet cable delivers both power and data, simplifying installation and improving reliability compared to Wi-Fi systems.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>ONVIF / RTSP</strong><br>Open standards that allow cross-brand camera compatibility.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>H.265 / H.265+</strong><br>Advanced compression formats that significantly reduce storage usage while maintaining video quality. H.265+ is becoming standard in 2026.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Hybrid NVR</strong><br>Supports both IP and analog cameras — ideal for upgrading older systems.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>AI / Smart Detection</strong><br>Motion, human, vehicle, and line-crossing detection. AI can be camera-side or NVR-side.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Key Factors to Consider When Choosing an NVR</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Number of Channels (Camera Support)</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The channel count determines how many cameras your NVR can support.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong><a href="http://Thereviews.github.io/4-Channel-NVR">4 channels</a></strong> → small apartments, shops, single-family homes</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong><a href="http://Thereviews.github.io/8-channel-NVR">8 channels</a></strong> → larger homes or future-proof setups</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong><a href="http://Thereviews.github.io/16-Channel-NVR">16</a>, <a href="http://Thereviews.github.io/32-NVR-Channel">32</a>, <a href="http://Thereviews.github.io/64-Channel-NVR">64</a> , <a href="http://Thereviews.github.io/128-Channel-NVR">128</a> channels</strong> → offices, warehouses, enterprise systems</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Tip: Always buy more channels than you currently need to allow future expansion.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Resolution Support</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Resolution impacts clarity and zoom capability.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>1080p / 2MP</strong> → Basic monitoring</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>4MP–5MP</strong> → Sharp and affordable balance</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>4K / 8MP+</strong> → Best clarity, zoom capability, future-proof</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>

<p>In 2026, 4K support is becoming the new standard for mid-range systems.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Storage &amp; Hard Drive Options</strong></h3>
