<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Choose a 64-Channel NVR</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Incoming Bandwidth</strong>: 320–400 Mbps+ is recommended for 64 cameras at 4K/12 MP resolution.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Built-In PoE</strong>: Some units include PoE ports; others require external PoE switches.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Max Camera Resolution</strong>: 12 MP+ is recommended for detailed monitoring.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Compression</strong>: H.265+ (HEVC) ensures storage efficiency and long retention.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>HDD Bays &amp; Storage Ceiling</strong>: Look for 8× SATA bays or more, with 80–128 TB+ support for continuous recording.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>RAID Support</strong>: RAID 0/1/5/6/10 with hot-swap options provides redundancy.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Multi-Monitor Output</strong>: Dual HDMI/VGA or 8K output is ideal for control rooms.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>AI / Smart Features</strong>: Facial recognition, people counting, license plate recognition, or motion detection.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Remote Access</strong>: Desktop, mobile, and web support for monitoring on the go.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Enterprise Considerations</strong>: NDAA compliance, robust build, and heat-dissipating metal chassis are important for professional setups.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
