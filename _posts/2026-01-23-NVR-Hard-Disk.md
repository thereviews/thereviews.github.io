<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Your NVR is only as reliable as the hard disk inside it. A surveillance-grade hard drive is designed for <strong>24/7 continuous recording</strong>, higher workloads, and smoother video write performance than regular desktop HDDs. Using the wrong drive can lead to dropped frames, corrupted footage, and early drive failure.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This guide reviews the <strong>best NVR hard disks</strong> based on endurance, recording stability, storage capacity, compatibility with popular NVR brands, and long-term reliability.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to Choose the Best NVR Hard Disk</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:table -->
<figure class="wp-block-table"><table class="has-fixed-layout"><thead><tr><th>Factor</th><th>Why it matters</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Surveillance Rating</td><td>Surveillance HDDs are optimized for continuous writes and multi-stream recording. Desktop drives wear out faster.</td></tr><tr><td>Capacity</td><td>2 TB ≈ 7–10 days of 4K footage with 4 cameras. More cameras or higher resolution = more storage needed.</td></tr><tr><td>Workload Rating</td><td>Measured in TB/year. Higher ratings mean better durability for 24/7 use.</td></tr><tr><td>Compatibility</td><td>Most NVRs support 3.5" SATA drives — verify max supported capacity before buying.</td></tr><tr><td>Warranty &amp; Reliability</td><td>Longer warranties usually indicate better endurance and build quality.</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>
