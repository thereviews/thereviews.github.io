<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Choose 16-Channel NVR</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>PoE Ports</strong>: 16 built-in PoE ports are ideal; 8 PoE + external switch support is fine if you plan to expand gradually.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Max Resolution</strong>: 4K / 8 MP minimum; 12–32 MP support is useful if future camera upgrades matter.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Incoming Bandwidth</strong> — 80–160 Mbps or higher ensures stable recording with multiple high-bitrate cameras.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Protocols</strong>: ONVIF S/T/G or RTSP allows cross-brand camera compatibility; proprietary systems are fine only if you won’t mix brands.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Compression</strong>: H.265+ or Ultra265 provides longer retention and lower network load.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>HDD Bays</strong>: Dual SATA bays (or more) are ideal for longer retention and redundancy.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Storage Ceiling</strong>: Support for 10–32 TB+ is important if long-term footage storage matters.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Remote Access</strong>: Mobile and desktop apps should be fast, stable, and free to use.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Fail-Safe Recording</strong>: Look for ANR or edge-sync features if uptime is mission-critical.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Build Quality</strong>: A metal chassis and good heat tolerance are important for 24/7 operation.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
