<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Good to Know Before Buying</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>All NVRs below record locally (no subscriptions required)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>PoE = one cable for power + video (simpler, more reliable)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Budget NVRs usually support fewer channels and less storage</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Wired NVR systems are more stable than Wi-Fi or battery cameras</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Choose the Best Cheap NVR</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>How many channels do you need?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>4 channels → apartments, small homes, shops</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>8 channels → larger homes or future expansion</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Always buy extra channels if you plan to add cameras later</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>What resolution should you support?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>4MP–5MP = good budget quality</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>4K (8MP) = better zoom and clarity</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Even if your cameras are lower-res now, future-proofing matters</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>How much storage is enough?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>2TB ≈ ~7–10 days for four 4K cameras (24/7)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Look for expandable HDD support if you want longer retention</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>PoE vs Hybrid NVRs</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>PoE NVRs → best for new IP camera installs</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Hybrid NVRs → best if upgrading an old analog system</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Compatibility</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>ONVIF support allows third-party cameras</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Brand-locked NVRs (like Reolink) work best within their ecosystem</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
