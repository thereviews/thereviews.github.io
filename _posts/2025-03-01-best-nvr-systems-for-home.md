<!-- wp:heading {"level":3} -->
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Choose the Best NVR Systems for Home</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Not all NVR systems are equally suited for home use. These are the most important factors homeowners should consider before buying.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Video Resolution &amp; Real-World Clarity</strong><br>While many systems advertise “4K,” true image quality depends on sensor size and bitrate, not just resolution. For identifying faces, packages, and vehicles around your home, true 8MP cameras with proper compression make a noticeable difference.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Smart Detection &amp; Alerts</strong><br>Basic motion detection can trigger dozens of false alerts from pets, shadows, or weather. Look for systems with person and vehicle detection, preferably processed locally on the NVR, which improves accuracy and avoids monthly fees.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Storage Capacity &amp; Retention Time<br>For typical home use</strong>:<br>2 TB = ~7–10 days of 4K recording with 4 cameras (continuous)<br>Expandable storage is important if you plan to add cameras or increase retention time</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Wired PoE vs Wi-Fi Cameras</strong><br>PoE systems are more reliable for homes because:<br>No batteries to replace<br>Less interference than Wi-Fi<br>More stable 24/7 recording<br>Wi-Fi cameras are convenient but less dependable for full-home security.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Ease of Installation &amp; App Experience</strong><br>Home users should prioritize:<br>Plug-and-play camera setup<br>Clear mobile playback<br>Simple alert controls<br>A confusing app can make even the best hardware frustrating to use.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->
