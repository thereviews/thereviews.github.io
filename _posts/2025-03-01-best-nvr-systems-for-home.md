<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>PoE vs. Wireless NVR Systems</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For most homeowners, <strong>PoE (Power over Ethernet)</strong> remains the best choice.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>PoE systems offer several advantages:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>One cable carries both power and video</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>More reliable connections</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Consistent image quality</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Lower latency</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Easier long-term maintenance</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Wireless NVR systems, however, are an excellent alternative when running Ethernet cables isn't practical.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Modern WiFi 6 systems, such as the xmartO Wireless AI Tracking NVR, deliver much stronger wireless performance than older Wi-Fi camera systems while still providing centralized local recording.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If reliability is your top priority, choose PoE.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If installation flexibility matters more, a high-quality wireless NVR can be the better option.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
