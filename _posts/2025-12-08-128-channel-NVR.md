<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to Choose a 128-Channel NVR</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Incoming Bandwidth</strong>:. Minimum 500–600 Mbps to prevent dropped frames across all cameras.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Storage Bays</strong>: 16+ SATA bays recommended for long-term retention at high resolutions.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>RAID Support</strong> Look for RAID 0/1/5/6/10 or N+M hot spare for redundancy and maximum uptime.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Resolution Support</strong>: Up to 12–32 MP for ultra-detailed monitoring; ensure camera specs match NVR capability.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Video Outputs</strong>: Dual 4K HDMI plus VGA for multi-monitor control room setups.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Smart Features</strong>: Facial recognition, human/vehicle detection, and ANPR for enterprise-level monitoring needs.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>PoE Support</strong>: Often requires separate PoE switches; verify camera power delivery compatibility.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Compression</strong>: H.265+ is strongly recommended to optimize storage efficiency.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Setup Complexity</strong>: Professional installation or IT expertise is advised for large-scale systems.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Brand &amp; Support</strong>: Choose established brands offering enterprise support and regular firmware updates.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
