<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Choose the Right Surveillance HDD</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>How much storage do you need?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>4–6TB → small home systems (2–4 cameras)</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>8–12TB → larger homes or small businesses</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Always leave headroom for future cameras</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Does RPM matter?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>5400 RPM → quieter, lower power, fine for small setups</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>7200 RPM → better for multiple HD/AI streams</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Do workload ratings matter?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>180 TB/year → standard 24/7 surveillance</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>300–550 TB/year → high-density, enterprise, or AI systems</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>Are surveillance features important?</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>Features like AllFrame, SkyHawk firmware, and RV sensors reduce dropped frames and vibration errors</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
