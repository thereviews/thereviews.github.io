<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Choose the Best ONVIF NVR</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:separator -->
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<!-- /wp:separator -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Check ONVIF profile support: </strong>At minimum Profile S; T and G are strong bonuses for events and storage tagging.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Ensure enough bandwidth: </strong>Higher ingest Mbps = smoother multi-cam recording (especially 4K+).</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Match channel count to your system: </strong>4-ch (small), 8-ch (mid-size), 16+ (commercial/large deployments).</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Look for efficient compression: </strong>Prefer H.265/H.265+ or Ultra 265 to maximize storage.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Storage capacity matters: </strong>Support for 6–10 TB is ideal for longer retention in 24/7 setups.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>RTSP support is a plus:</strong> Adds even more flexibility alongside ONVIF for third-party cams.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>PoE is optional, not required:</strong> Useful for easy install but doesn’t affect ONVIF compatibility.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Remote access security: </strong>QR/app setup is good, but check for encrypted logins and network isolation options.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Playback needs:</strong> Some NVRs limit simultaneous playback, choose based on monitoring workflow.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Scalability vs simplicity: </strong>Software NVR (Blue Iris) scales more; hardware NVR is easier and more stable.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
