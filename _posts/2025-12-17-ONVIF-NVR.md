<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>An ONVIF-compatible NVR is the best choice if you want maximum flexibility when building or expanding a security camera system. Unlike brand-locked NVRs, ONVIF NVRs work with cameras from multiple manufacturers, allowing you to mix and match models without replacing your entire setup. We reviewed the best ONVIF NVRs based on compatibility, stability, recording performance, and ease of use to help you choose the right system.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading -->
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How to Choose the Best ONVIF NVR</strong></h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Choosing the right ONVIF NVR depends on a few critical factors:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>ONVIF Profile Support:</strong> Look for ONVIF Profile S and T for video streaming and Profile G for edge storage support.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Camera Compatibility:</strong> A good ONVIF NVR should work reliably with third-party IP cameras without constant reconfiguration.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Recording Performance:</strong> Smooth 24/7 recording, fast playback, and stable stream handling are essential.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Storage &amp; Expandability:</strong> Support for large hard drives (at least 2 TB) and expandable storage ensures longer retention.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Ease of Management:</strong> A clean interface, mobile app access, and remote monitoring make daily use easier.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->
